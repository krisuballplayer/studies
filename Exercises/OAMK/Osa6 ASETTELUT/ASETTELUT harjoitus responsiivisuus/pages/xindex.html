Seasons in Finland

The annual changes in temperature are of crucial importance for Finland's climate. The stark contrasts between the four seasons are the main characteristics of the annual natural cycle in Finland.

Winter

Permanent snow covers open grounds about two weeks after winter begins. The snow cover is deepest around mid-March, with an average of 60 to 90 cm of snow in eastern and northern Finland and 20 to 30 cm in southwestern Finland. The lakes freeze over in late November and early December.

Spring

Ice melting and birds singing – these are the sounds of Finnish spring.

Summer

The summer isn’t endless in Finland, but there are almost endless summer days. On a fine summer day, Finns have an overwhelming urge to go outside and get out of town.

Autumn

The growing season ends in autumn when the mean daily temperature drops below 5°C. This occurs around the last week of September in northern Finland and in late October or early November in southwestern Finland.
